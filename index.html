Colin Lea

I am a senior PhD student in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins focused on fine-grained action recognition in complex domains. I am applications-driven and care about practical vision/machine learning problems. In recent years my work has spanned surgical phase modeling, automated monitoring in ICUs, industrial manufacturing, and beyond.

My technical focus is on three important aspects of activity modeling. First, how can we develop better spatiotemporal representations of what constitutes an action. Second, how can we better leverage the temporal structure of a sequence of actions in video. Lastly, how can we use auxiliary sensor data (e.g. robot kinematics) in the training process to learn better spatiotemporal models.

My PhD committee is composed of Greg Hager, Rene Vidal, and Austin Reiter. In the past I was graciously funded as an NSF Graduate Research Fellow (2012-2015) and an Intuitive Surgical Fellow (2011-2012).
