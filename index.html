I am a 5th year PhD student in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University who works on fine-grained action recognition in complex domains. Some application areas include surgical workflow analysis, activity recognition in intensive care units, cooking tasks, and collaborative manufacturing. At Hopkins, I am part of the Laboratory for Computational Sensing and Robotics co-advised by Greg Hager and Rene Vidal. I spend much of my time thinking about time series models (e.g. CRFs, Temporal CNNs, Segmental Models) for video and multi-modal sensor data. I am a NSF Graduate Research Fellow and an Intuitive Surgical Fellow.
