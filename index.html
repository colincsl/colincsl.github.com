Colin Lea

I am a researcher at Apple where I work on machine learning for accessibility within the AI/ML org. I focus on developing new technologies for individuals with speech or motor-speech disorders.

I spent 2017 through 2019 at Facebook Reality Labs where I worked on animating avatars in real-time from multimodal sensing for potential applications in VR-based telepresence. See this page for a cool look at our group's work on photorealistic modeling of virtual humans.

In late 2016, I defended my PhD in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University where I developed multimodal models for fine-grained action segmentation with my primary advisor Greg Hager and co-advisors Rene Vidal and Austin Reiter. During my tenure at Hopkins I worked on applications in surgery, ICUs/healthcare, industrial manufacturing, and beyond.
